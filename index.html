About Me

In 2014, I began my new career in Tech Support, and then expanded into coding over the successive years. I've taken the U of Minnesota Coding Bootcamp Program and completed classes in Java, Python and discrete math at Metro State University.

In Java, we covered heaps, stacks, polymorphism, encapsulation and I still dream of "public static main voids."
I speak a bit of Japanese; I can read Hiragana and Katakana, but I know a bit more of Spanish than Japanese. Also, I'm a licensed foster parent with Hennepin County. Once upon my past, I used to do glamorous things like "Go to Spin Class", "Read actual books", and that thing they call "naps".

My new hobbies include managing Type 1 diabetes in a toddler and navigating the meetings foster parents must attend for the county.
